The Wonders Of Natural Pest Repellents

Pesticidal properties of marigolds

Marigolds contain a certain scent called “alpha-terpineol,” which makes insects find them unappetizing. They also have a fragrance similar to basil, which insects don’t like to feed on.

The African Marigold has the highest amount of phytochemicals, which is the type of fragrance that wards off insects. This also wards off bunnies, deer, and elk.

There are about 52 natural pest repellent plants, but marigolds can be useful and can ward off lots of things that normal pesticides do just as well while being a very eco-friendly pesticide. Marigolds contain a citrus-like scent in concentrations of 5-15%. This natural aroma is powerful enough to repel pesky insects such as mosquitoes, flies, and ticks.

  • Garlic: A gardener's secret weapon, garlic is a natural deterrent for a wide variety of pests. Its pungent aroma makes it unpleasant for many critters, from aphids to ants.
  • Mint: Not only does mint add a fresh aroma to your garden, but it also repels insects.
  • Baking Soda: A kitchen staple with surprising garden benefits, baking soda creates an environment that is inhospitable to slugs, garden pests, and red ants.

Other Pest Repellents

There are lots of other plants that work as natural pest repellents. One of the other plants is mint. Mint does the same thing as marigolds but is invasive, growing everywhere when one is already planted.
